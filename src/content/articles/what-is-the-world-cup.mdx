## The basic idea

The FIFA World Cup is the biggest tournament in soccer. It happens every four years and features national teams from around the world competing for the most prestigious trophy in the sport.

No other sporting event draws as many viewers globally. The final alone can attract over a billion people watching.

## How teams qualify

Qualification takes place over several years before the tournament. Each continent has its own qualification process run by its confederation.

Teams play a series of matches in groups or rounds. Only a limited number of spots are available, so the competition starts long before the main event.

## The tournament format

The World Cup starts with a group stage. Teams are placed into groups of four and play each other once. The top two teams from each group advance to the knockout rounds.

From there, it is single elimination. Losers go home. The final match decides the world champion.

## Why it matters to US fans

The US men's national team has been a regular participant in recent decades. Hosting duties are also coming to North America in 2026, with matches in the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

The women's team has been even more successful, winning multiple World Cup titles and becoming a major force in the sport.

## How it differs from club soccer

The World Cup is about national pride, not club loyalty. Players leave their clubs to represent their countries. This can create unique chemistry issues since national teams train together far less than club teams do.

The tournament also has a different tactical feel. Teams often play more conservatively because the stakes are so high and there is less time to build cohesion.

## What to watch

Look for how smaller nations compete against traditional powers. Upsets happen regularly in the World Cup, and they are part of what makes the tournament special.

Also pay attention to the atmosphere. Fans travel from all over the world, and the stadiums become a showcase of global soccer culture.
