The UEFA Champions League is the biggest club football competition on the planet. But how exactly does the format work? If you've ever been confused about the league phase, knockout rounds, or how teams qualify, this guide breaks it all down in simple terms.

## What Is the Champions League?

The Champions League is an annual tournament run by UEFA (the governing body of European football). The best clubs from across Europe compete to be crowned continental champions. It's been running since 1955 and is widely considered the most prestigious club trophy in the sport.

## How Do Teams Qualify?

Qualification depends on a country's **UEFA coefficient ranking** — a system that measures how well clubs from each nation perform in European competitions over a five-year period.

Here's how it generally works:

- **Top-ranked countries** (like England, Spain, Germany, Italy) get **four automatic group-stage spots**. These go to the clubs that finish in the top four of their domestic league.
- **Mid-ranked countries** (like France, Netherlands, Portugal) get **two or three spots**, depending on their coefficient.
- **Lower-ranked countries** get **one or two spots**, often requiring teams to go through multiple qualifying rounds before reaching the main tournament.

Some spots are also reserved for the **Europa League title holders** and **domestic cup winners** from certain nations.

## The New League Phase (Since 2024–25)

UEFA revamped the Champions League starting from the 2024–25 season. The old group stage with eight groups of four teams was replaced with a **single league phase** involving **36 teams**.

Here's how it works:

- Each team plays **eight matches** against eight different opponents (four home, four away).
- Opponents are drawn based on seeding pots, so every team faces a mix of strong and weaker sides.
- All 36 teams are ranked in **one big league table** based on points (3 for a win, 1 for a draw, 0 for a loss).

### What Happens After the League Phase?

The league table determines who advances:

- **Top 8** → Qualify directly for the **Round of 16**.
- **9th to 24th** → Enter a **knockout playoff round** (two-legged ties) to earn a spot in the Round of 16.
- **25th to 36th** → **Eliminated** from the Champions League. Some may drop into the Europa League depending on their position.

## The Knockout Rounds

Once the league phase and playoffs are done, the tournament follows a traditional knockout bracket:

1. **Round of 16** — Two-legged ties (home and away)
2. **Quarter-finals** — Two-legged ties
3. **Semi-finals** — Two-legged ties
4. **Final** — Single match at a neutral venue

In all two-legged knockout ties, the team with the **higher aggregate score** advances. If the aggregate is level, the match goes to **extra time** and then **penalties** if needed.

## The Final

The Champions League final is a single match held at a **pre-selected neutral venue** — typically one of Europe's biggest stadiums. It's one of the most-watched sporting events in the world, regularly drawing hundreds of millions of viewers.

The winner earns:

- The Champions League trophy
- A spot in the **FIFA Club World Cup**
- A place in the **UEFA Super Cup**
- Automatic qualification for next season's Champions League (regardless of domestic league finish)

## How Is the Draw Conducted?

The league phase draw uses a **Swiss-system format** with four seeding pots. Each team plays:

- Two opponents from **Pot 1**
- Two opponents from **Pot 2**
- Two opponents from **Pot 3**
- Two opponents from **Pot 4**

This ensures balanced but varied matchups. The draw is done by computer algorithm to meet all constraints.

## Common Questions

### Can a team from a small country win the Champions League?

It's extremely rare. The competition is heavily weighted toward clubs from top leagues due to their larger revenue, better squads, and more qualifying spots. However, teams like Porto (2004) and Red Star Belgrade (1991) have pulled off surprise wins.

### Do teams get prize money?

Yes. UEFA distributes hundreds of millions of euros in prize money. Even just reaching the league phase earns a club significant revenue. The further a team progresses, the more they earn.

### What's the difference between the Champions League and Europa League?

The Champions League is the top-tier European competition. The Europa League is the second tier, featuring teams that finish lower in their domestic leagues or drop down from the Champions League. UEFA also runs a third-tier competition called the **Conference League**.

### Why do some teams play extra qualifying rounds?

Teams from lower-ranked countries enter earlier because their domestic leagues are considered less competitive by UEFA's coefficient system. They must prove themselves through multiple qualifying rounds before facing the big clubs.

## Key Takeaways

- 36 teams now compete in a **single league phase** instead of traditional groups.
- Each team plays **eight matches** against different opponents.
- The top 8 go straight to the Round of 16; 9th–24th enter a playoff.
- Knockout rounds are **two-legged** until the final, which is a single match.
- The format rewards consistency — every match matters in the league table.
