England produced one of the most extraordinary World Cup matches in history, beating France 6-4 in the third-place play-off to secure their best finish at the tournament since winning it in 1966.

Bukayo Saka scored a stunning hat-trick as England raced to a 4-0 half-time lead, only for France to fight back to 4-3 before the Three Lions held on in a breathless contest in Miami.

## A First-Half Masterclass

England came out flying from the first whistle. Captain Declan Rice gave them the lead after just three minutes, firing a right-footed shot from outside the box into the bottom corner.

The floodgates appeared to open. Ezri Konsa headed in from a Rice corner on 18 minutes to double the advantage. Then Saka took over.

The Arsenal winger scored twice before half-time — first on 37 minutes with a curling left-footed effort from the left side of the box, then again in first-half stoppage time after a perfectly weighted through ball from Eberechi Eze.

At 4-0, the match appeared over. England assistant manager Anthony Barry was visibly emotional during his half-time interview with BBC One. "I can't find the words to describe how proud I am of these players," he said, his voice breaking. "They're playing a game with broken hearts."

## France's Furious Comeback

Whatever Didier Deschamps said at half-time worked. Kylian Mbappé pulled one back just three minutes into the second half, latching onto a Michael Olise through ball to make it 4-1.

Bradley Barcola added a second on 54 minutes after a Mbappé assist, and suddenly the momentum had shifted entirely. When Mbappé scored again on 66 minutes — his second of the match, also assisted by Olise — France trailed by just one goal at 4-3.

The stadium was electric. France pressed for an equaliser as England's defence, so commanding in the first half, began to wobble under relentless French pressure.

## Saka Completes His Hat-Trick

With the match hanging in the balance, England won a penalty on 87 minutes. Saka stepped up and calmly slotted the ball into the bottom right corner to complete his hat-trick and give England breathing room at 5-3.

It was a moment of redemption for Saka, whose tournament had been overshadowed by the debate around Thomas Tuchel's decision to leave him out of the semi-final against Argentina. His three-goal performance against France was a emphatic answer to his critics.

## A Chaotic Finale

The drama was far from over. Ousmane Dembélé scored in the sixth minute of stoppage time to make it 5-4, setting up a frantic finish. But Jude Bellingham had the final word, racing clear on a fast break in the 98th minute to seal the 6-4 victory.

The goal was Bellingham's seventh of the tournament — a new England record for goals at a single World Cup. The Real Madrid midfielder has been one of the standout players of the 2026 edition.

## What It Means for England

England's third-place finish is their best at a World Cup in 60 years. Rice, who captained the side with Harry Kane an unused substitute, was defiant about the team's trajectory.

"This is the best England group for a very long time. That's a fact," Rice said. "We're tired of saying we're proud of coming in semi-finals and quarter-finals — we want to win with England ultimately. But to come third in this tournament is a real achievement."

Despite the backlash to his semi-final tactical decisions, Tuchel is expected to stay as manager for Euro 2028. The German acknowledged the pain of the Argentina defeat but insisted the squad had not lost faith.

"We are ferociously competitive, so we almost don't allow ourselves to be proud of third place because we set the highest goal," Tuchel said. "The scar will stay. This is how it is in high-level sport."

## Looking Ahead

For France, the fourth-place finish represents a disappointing end to a tournament where they had been among the favourites. Mbappé's brace took his tournament tally to 10 goals, putting him firmly in the Golden Boot race ahead of the final between Spain and Argentina.

For England, the challenge now is to translate this progress into tournament victories. With Euro 2028 on home soil, the opportunity to end their long wait for a major trophy is tantalisingly close.
