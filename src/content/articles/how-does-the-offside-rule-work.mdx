## What is offside?

A player is in an offside position if they are closer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-to-last defender at the moment the ball is played to them. The goalkeeper usually counts as one of the last two defenders.

Being in an offside position by itself is not a foul. It only becomes an offense if the player actively involves themselves in play from that position.

## Why the rule exists

The offside rule prevents players from camping near the opponent's goal and waiting for a long pass. Without it, attackers could stand by the goalkeeper all game and wait for a simple tap-in.

The rule encourages movement, passing, and tactical buildup play.

## When is it called?

The assistant referee (the flag person on the sideline) watches for offside at the exact moment a teammate passes the ball. If an attacker is in an offside position and then touches the ball, interferes with an opponent, or gains an advantage from that position, the referee calls offside.

The result is an indirect free kick for the defending team.

## Common misunderstandings

- **You can be offside on a corner kick or goal kick.** No. Offside is not called on corners, goal kicks, or throw-ins.
- **If you are level with the last defender, you are offside.** No. Being level with the second-to-last defender is not offside.
- **The flag always means offside.** Sometimes the flag goes up late or VAR gets involved. The final call comes from the referee.

## How VAR changed things

Video Assistant Referee (VAR) reviews have made offside calls more precise but also more controversial. Tight calls are now judged by freeze-frame lines drawn on the screen.

This has led to goals being disallowed by very small margins, which frustrates fans and players alike.

## What to watch for

Watch how attacking players time their runs to stay onside. Good strikers know exactly when to sprint past the defensive line.

When you see a goal disallowed for offside, look at the replay to see where the attacker was when the pass was made. That timing is everything.
