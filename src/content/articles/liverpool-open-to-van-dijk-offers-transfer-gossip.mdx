The summer transfer window is heating up, and Saturday's gossip columns are packed with major stories. The biggest headline? Liverpool are reportedly prepared to listen to offers for their captain, Virgil van Dijk.

## Liverpool Willing to Sell Van Dijk

According to reports from Teamtalk, Liverpool are open to offers for Dutch centre-back Virgil van Dijk this summer. The 34-year-old signed a contract extension last year, but that decision is now viewed with regret by some figures inside Anfield.

Van Dijk has been the backbone of Liverpool's defence since arriving from Southampton in 2018 for a then-world-record fee for a defender. He helped the club win the Champions League in 2019 and the Premier League title in 2020.

However, age and recent form have raised questions about his long-term future at the club. If Liverpool do receive a suitable offer, it could mark the end of an era at Anfield.

## Fulham Join Race for Summerville

Fulham have entered the competition to sign West Ham winger Crysencio Summerville. The 24-year-old Netherlands international is also attracting interest from Chelsea and Manchester United, according to the Guardian.

Summerville impressed during his time at Leeds United before making the move to West Ham. His pace and direct running style make him an attractive option for several Premier League clubs looking to strengthen their attacking options.

## Inter Milan Target Chelsea's Chalobah

Chelsea centre-back Trevoh Chalobah could be heading to Italy. According to Gazzetta dello Sport, the 26-year-old's agent has held meetings with Inter Milan representatives over a potential move to Serie A.

Italian club Como are also said to be interested in the defender, who has struggled for regular playing time at Stamford Bridge. A move to Italy could offer Chalobah the consistent minutes he needs to develop further.

- **Manchester City** teenager Matty Warhurst, 19, is attracting interest from Stockport County, Northampton Town, and Swindon Town

## What to Watch

The Van Dijk situation is the one to keep an eye on. If Liverpool truly are open to selling their captain, it would signal a significant shift in the club's defensive rebuild. Meanwhile, the battle for Summerville between three Premier League clubs could become one of the sagas of the summer.

With the World Cup still ongoing, expect more transfer activity as clubs look to finalise their squads before the new season begins.
