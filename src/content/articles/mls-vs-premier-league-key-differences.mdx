## Closed league vs open pyramid

MLS is a closed league. Clubs do not get relegated for finishing near the bottom, and teams enter the league through expansion rather than promotion.

The Premier League sits at the top of an open English pyramid. Clubs can be promoted into it and relegated out of it.

## Playoffs vs table champion

MLS uses playoffs to decide its champion. The regular season matters for seeding, home-field advantage, and qualification, but MLS Cup decides the title.

The Premier League champion is the club with the most points after 38 league matches. There is no playoff for the league title.

## Salary rules

MLS has a salary budget, Designated Players, allocation money, and other roster tools. These rules are designed to control spending and maintain competitive balance.

The Premier League does not use the same kind of centralized salary cap. Clubs still face financial regulations, but wealthy clubs can generally spend more on wages and transfers.

## Transfers and squad building

Premier League clubs buy and sell players in a global transfer market. MLS clubs do that too, but MLS roster rules add extra layers.

That is why an MLS signing can involve transfer fees, international roster spots, salary budget charges, and Designated Player status at the same time.

## Which one is better?

They are built for different environments. MLS is shaped by American sports business logic, travel distances, expansion, and parity. The Premier League is shaped by more than a century of English club competition and global media power.

Understanding those structural differences makes both leagues easier to follow.
