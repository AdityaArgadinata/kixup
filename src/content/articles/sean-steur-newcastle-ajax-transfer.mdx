Newcastle United are closing in on the signing of Ajax midfielder Sean Steur, with a deal worth up to £23m nearing completion. The 18-year-old is set to become the club's latest addition in a busy summer transfer window.

## Who Is Sean Steur?

Sean Steur is an 18-year-old central midfielder who came through Ajax's famous academy system. He made his first-team debut in December 2025 and quickly became a regular in the starting lineup.

One of his standout moments came in the Champions League, where he became the youngest Ajax player ever to record an assist in the competition, at just 17 years and 333 days old.

SteuR also started in Ajax's De Klassieker victory over Feyenoord, showing the coaching staff trusted him in high-pressure matches despite his young age.

## Why Newcastle Want Him

Newcastle have been looking to strengthen their midfield options, and Steur's profile fits what the club want: young, technically gifted, and capable of developing further under Eddie Howe.

The numbers back up the eye test. Among midfielders aged 18 or younger who started the 2025-26 Eredivisie season, Steur ranked:

- **First** for chances created (15)
- **First** for total carries (231)
- **First** for duel success rate (56.8%)
- **Second** for passes completed (623)
- **Second** for passing accuracy (89.7%)
- **Second** for tackles made (20)

These are impressive figures for any midfielder, let alone a teenager playing his first full season of senior football.

## The Tonali Connection

The timing of this move is directly linked to Sandro Tonali's departure. Tottenham completed the signing of Tonali from Newcastle for a club-record fee that could reach £100m, giving the Magpies significant funds to reinvest.

Newcastle have already spent £43m on Hoffenheim winger Bazoumana Toure earlier in the window. The Steur deal, at around £23m, would take their summer spending to roughly £66m — still leaving room for further signings.

## Ajax's Position

SteuR signed a new contract with Ajax last summer, but that deal runs only until 2028. With two years remaining, Ajax are in a somewhat vulnerable negotiating position.

The Dutch club would prefer to keep their academy graduate, but the combination of a strong offer from Newcastle and the player's desire to test himself in the Premier League may prove decisive.

## What Steur Brings to Newcastle

SteuR is a modern central midfielder who combines technical quality with physical competitiveness. His ability to carry the ball forward, create chances, and win duels makes him a well-rounded option in the middle of the park.

At 18, he would not be expected to start every match immediately. Instead, he could develop gradually under Howe's guidance, learning from experienced midfielders while contributing in cup competitions and rotation appearances.

## What to Watch

The deal is expected to be completed in the coming days. If finalized, Steur would join a Newcastle squad preparing for Champions League football next season.

Keep an eye on how quickly Howe integrates the teenager into the first-team setup. Ajax have a strong track record of producing midfielders who thrive in the Premier League, and Steur's early career numbers suggest he could follow that path.
