Spain are world champions once again. A single goal from Ferran Torres in extra time was enough to seal a 1-0 victory over Argentina in the 2026 World Cup final at the New York New Jersey Stadium on Sunday.

## How the Match Unfolded

Spain controlled the game from the opening whistle. They pressed Argentina high, kept the ball, and created the better chances across 90 minutes. Argentina, the defending champions, struggled to find their usual rhythm against Spain's relentless possession game.

The first half ended goalless, with Spain enjoying the majority of the ball but unable to break through Argentina's defence. Lionel Messi and his teammates looked dangerous on the counter, but Spain's backline held firm.

The second half followed the same pattern. Spain pushed forward, Argentina sat deep and tried to hit on the break. Neither side could find the breakthrough in normal time, sending the final to extra time for the first time since 2010.

## Ferran Torres: The Decisive Moment

The breakthrough came in extra time. Ferran Torres, who had been a constant threat throughout the tournament, found the back of the net to give Spain the lead. It was the goal that would decide the World Cup.

Torres finished the tournament as one of Spain's most important players. His pace, movement, and clinical finishing proved too much for Argentina's defence in the decisive moment.

Argentina pushed for an equaliser in the closing stages, but Spain held firm. When the final whistle blew, the Spanish players collapsed in celebration. They had done it — a second World Cup title, eight years after their first in 2010.

## Rodri Wins the Golden Ball

Spain's Rodri was named the tournament's best player, winning the Golden Ball. The midfielder was the heartbeat of Spain's campaign, controlling games with his passing, positioning, and intelligence.

Rodri's performances throughout the tournament were outstanding. He dictated the tempo in every match, broke up opposition attacks, and kept Spain's midfield ticking. It was a deserved award for one of the most complete midfielders in world football.

## Mbappe Makes History with Golden Boot

France's Kylian Mbappe won the Golden Boot as the tournament's top scorer, becoming the first player in history to win the award twice. Mbappe also claimed it at the 2022 World Cup in Qatar.

Despite France losing to Spain in the semi-finals, Mbappe's goal record throughout the tournament was enough to secure the award. His pace and finishing remained lethal throughout the competition.

## What This Means for Spain

Spain's second World Cup title confirms their status as one of football's great national teams. After winning in 2010 with Xavi and Iniesta, they have now won again with a new generation led by Rodri, Pedri, and Ferran Torres.

The victory is also a vindication of Spain's style of play. Their possession-based approach, often questioned in recent years, proved decisive on the biggest stage. They controlled matches, limited opponents' chances, and took their opportunities when they came.

For Argentina, it is a painful defeat. Lionel Messi, now 39, was playing in what is likely his final World Cup. He came agonisingly close to defending the title he won in 2022, but it was not to be.

## What to Watch Next

With the World Cup now over, attention turns to the new domestic season. The Premier League, La Liga, and other European leagues will resume in the coming weeks. Many of the players who starred in this tournament will be back in action for their clubs.

The 2026 World Cup will be remembered as a tournament of drama, late goals, and ultimately, Spanish dominance. Spain proved that patient, intelligent football can still win at the highest level.
