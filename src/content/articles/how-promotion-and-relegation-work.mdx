## The basic idea

Promotion and relegation is the system that moves clubs between divisions based on performance. Good teams in a lower division move up. Poor teams in a higher division move down.

This is one of the biggest differences between European soccer and major American sports leagues.

## Promotion

Promotion rewards successful lower-division teams. If a club finishes near the top of its league, it can earn a place in the division above it for the next season.

In England, for example, clubs can climb from the Championship into the Premier League. That jump can be worth a huge amount of money because of Premier League broadcast revenue.

## Relegation

Relegation punishes poor performance. If a club finishes near the bottom of the standings, it drops into the division below for the next season.

That creates pressure throughout the table. A team near last place is not just losing games. It may be fighting to protect revenue, player contracts, and the future of the club.

## Why American fans notice it

Most US leagues are closed leagues. Teams do not lose their place in the league because of a bad season. In European soccer, the bottom of the standings can be as dramatic as the top.

That is why fans often talk about the relegation battle. Survival can feel like a championship for clubs trying to stay up.

## Why it changes incentives

Promotion and relegation affects coaching decisions, transfer spending, and match strategy. A club may play cautiously late in the season because one point could be the difference between survival and relegation.

It also gives smaller clubs a dream. In theory, a well-run club can climb the ladder and reach the top level.
