Aston Villa have completed the signing of midfielder Johan Manzambi from Freiburg for a club record fee of just over £50 million. The Switzerland international becomes Unai Emery's first major signing of the summer.

## Who Is Johan Manzambi?

Manzambi is a 20-year-old central midfielder who has rapidly risen through the ranks at Freiburg. Born in Switzerland, he represented his country at the 2026 World Cup, scoring three goals as Switzerland reached the quarter-finals before falling to Argentina.

His performances in North America caught the attention of several top European clubs. Villa moved quickly to secure his signature after Newcastle had initially agreed a fee with Freiburg.

## What Villa Are Getting

The numbers tell the story of a breakout season. Manzambi scored seven goals in 47 appearances across all competitions last term as Freiburg finished seventh in the Bundesliga. He was named Europa League Young Player of the Season after helping the German side reach the final in Istanbul.

That Europa League final is an interesting footnote. Manzambi played against Villa in that match, with Emery's side winning 3-0 to end a 30-year trophy drought. Clearly, the Villa manager liked what he saw from the young midfielder that night.

## The Transfer Fee

At just over £50 million, Manzambi becomes Villa's most expensive signing ever. The fee surpasses the £50 million Villa paid Everton for Amadou Onana two years ago.

The price tag reflects both Manzambi's potential and the current market. Young midfielders with international pedigree and European experience command premium fees. Villa clearly believe the investment will pay off over the long term.

## Why Villa Needed Him

Villa's midfield has undergone significant changes this summer. Youri Tielemans joined Manchester United for £35 million after the Old Trafford club triggered his release clause. Amadou Onana suffered a serious knee injury at the World Cup with Belgium and is not expected to return until next year.

Those two departures left Emery short of options in the centre of the park. Manzambi's arrival addresses that gap immediately.

Villa are also closing in on Wolves midfielder Joao Gomes for £38 million, signalling a major midfield rebuild ahead of the new season.

## The Bigger Picture

Villa's spending is not without risk. The club must comply with UEFA financial regulations, and their finances are described as tight. Left-back Lucas Digne is close to rejoining Paris Saint-Germain, which would bring in some funds.

But the Manzambi deal shows Villa's ambition. They are building a squad capable of competing in the Premier League and in Europe. Signing a 20-year-old who starred at a World Cup and in the Europa League is a statement of intent.

## What to Watch

- **Adaptation to the Premier League** — Moving from the Bundesliga to England's top flight is a significant step. Manzambi's physicality and technical ability will be tested immediately.
- **Partnership with Joao Gomes** — If Villa complete the Gomes signing, the two could form a dynamic central midfield pairing.
- **Europa League return** — Villa will compete in Europe again next season. Manzambi's continental experience will be valuable.
- **World Cup momentum** — Three goals at the World Cup is a strong return. Can he carry that form into the Premier League?

Villa have made a bold move. Whether Manzambi justifies the club record fee will depend on how quickly he adapts to English football, but the early signs are promising.
