## Why MLS has special roster rules

MLS uses roster rules to manage spending and encourage competitive balance. The league has grown inside the American sports market, so its structure borrows ideas from US leagues while still operating in the global soccer market.

That creates a system that can feel complicated at first.

## Salary budget

The salary budget is the baseline spending framework for MLS rosters. Not every dollar of a player's pay counts the same way against the budget, which is where the details begin.

The league publishes roster rules, and those rules can change over time.

## Designated Players

The Designated Player rule allows MLS clubs to sign higher-cost players whose full compensation does not all count against the salary budget.

This is how clubs can sign stars while still operating under league roster limits.

## Allocation money

Allocation money is a roster-building tool that clubs can use to reduce certain salary budget charges or complete eligible moves.

For fans, the important point is simple: allocation money gives clubs flexibility inside a controlled spending system.

## Why it matters

MLS roster rules shape which players clubs can sign, how teams balance stars with depth, and why some deals take longer than expected.

Understanding the basics makes MLS transfer news much easier to read.
